💎 Hidden gems at Kaiyukan — things most visitors miss

Feeding times — check schedule at entrance immediately:
• Penguin feeding: 10:20 AM and 2:30 PM
• Seal feeding: 10:20 AM and 3:05 PM
• Otter feeding: 11:30 AM, 1:30 PM, 3:30 PM
• Whale shark feeding: 2–3x daily, posted at Pacific Ocean tank — crowds gather 10–15 min before

Touch pool (near the end of the route) — touch small sharks, stingrays, and fish. He can do this unassisted. Hugo touching a small shark is a proper story.

Jellyfish Galaxy zone — jellyfish in space-like dark lighting. Genuinely mesmerizing for kids and adults.

Café inside the aquarium — sit with a view of the Pacific Ocean tank while eating. Whale shark soft serve (ramune + vanilla, inspired by the shark's pattern). Worth the stop mid-visit.

Backstage feeding tickets (¥2,400–4,700) — buy online or at door for a behind-the-scenes view of feeding. You've confirmed you want this — see booking checklist.

⏰
Time-sensitive — Kaiyukan backstage feeding
Backstage feeding tickets sell out, especially on weekdays in April. Book online via the Kaiyukan official site before the trip. Two experience tiers: Whale Shark feeding (¥4,700/person — most popular, sells out first) and smaller animal feeding (¥2,400 — seals, rays, etc). Decide which you want and book at the same time as your entry tickets. He is old enough for both — the whale shark one is the one worth paying for.
⏰
Day-of timing strategy — plan your route around feedings
Pick up the daily feeding schedule at the entrance desk the moment you arrive. The spiral descent takes ~2 hrs naturally. Key move: if Whale Shark feeding is at 2 PM, slow down your descent so you arrive at the Pacific Ocean tank (Floor 3–4) at 1:45 PM. If Penguin feeding is at 10:20 AM, head there first before the main spiral. You can't go back up, so plan the sequence before you start.
★ Memory moment

Stand at the Pacific Ocean tank as the whale shark passes — it's floor-to-ceiling glass and you can watch from multiple levels as you spiral down. Pick the level where you're at eye height with the whale shark. Hugo realising how big it is relative to himself is one of those silently overwhelming moments.

🌿 For James

Kaiyukan's design is genuinely exceptional — each of the 15 tanks represents a specific Pacific Rim ecosystem. The Japanese Spider Crab tank (enormous), the Antarctic section (penguins above and below ice), the Aleutian Islands section with sea otters. This is a world-class nature experience, not just a kids attraction.
